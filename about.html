About Appliance Repair Handbook

A service center built on honesty, technical expertise, and a genuine commitment to helping homeowners get more life from their appliances.

Our Story

15 Years Fixing What Others Replace

Appliance Repair Handbook started in 2009 when founder James Hartwell noticed a troubling pattern: homeowners were being told their appliances were "beyond repair" when, in fact, they needed a $30 part and 45 minutes of skilled labor. The goal from day one was simple — honest diagnosis, transparent pricing, and repairs that last.

What began as a one-truck operation in a single metro area has grown into a team of 14 certified technicians serving residential and light commercial clients. We've documented over 38,000 repairs across every major appliance category and brand, and we've distilled that knowledge into the free guides on this site.

Our editorial team — working technicians with an average of 12 years of field experience — writes and maintains every guide on this site. We don't publish anything we haven't tested in the field. When we recommend replacing a thermal fuse before a heating element, it's because we've diagnosed hundreds of dryers and know that's the right sequence. This is real-world experience, not content farm output.

Our Commitment

What We Believe

Honest Diagnosis

We never recommend a repair that isn't justified. If your appliance is genuinely not worth repairing, we'll tell you. We'd rather lose a repair job than put $300 of labor into a machine that will fail again in six months.

Transparent Pricing

You receive a written estimate before any work begins. The final invoice will match the estimate unless additional faults are discovered mid-repair — and we'll always call you before proceeding with any unanticipated work.

Right to Repair

We strongly support homeowners' right to repair their own appliances. Our free guides are written to genuinely help, not to funnel you into paid service. If a repair is within your capability, we'll tell you that too.

Environmental Responsibility

Repairing an appliance instead of replacing it typically saves 200–800 lbs of CO₂ emissions from new manufacturing. We partner with certified recyclers for all components we replace, including proper refrigerant recovery and disposal.
